# Dark Mirror Profile: For the Promise (Book 2)

> **Provenance**: Plain text = canon from `source-roadmap.md`. **[PROPOSED]** =
> assistant suggestion. **[TBD]** = needs author decision.

## Status: UNDECIDED **[TBD]**

The roadmap attests three antagonistic forces but does not name Book 2's Dark
Mirror. The Crucible Structure wants one antagonist who is the protagonist's
shadow — same gift or same wound, different choice.

## Candidate 1: Caelus (Scaleblade) — mirror to Elaine

- Canon: "Elaine's direct Scaleblade foil" (author's words, Open Threads)
- Canon: possibly the void she felt at the gate — his influence rather than
  Celisia's nature
- **[PROPOSED]** shape: if Elaine's mark is receiving-without-consent (every
  room floods her), a foil whose power is *erasing* presence — a void where a
  person should be — mirrors her perfectly: she cannot stop knowing; he cannot
  be known. Their convergence would be the fire strand's climax.

## Candidate 2: Tauvor (Archwarden) — mirror in method

- Canon: confesses a disaster he caused while controlling the narrative;
  genuine fear beneath composure; withholds the truth from the people he's
  hiring to walk into it
- **[PROPOSED]** shape: Tauvor and Elaine both live by managing what others
  are allowed to know. She withholds to protect; he withholds to control. The
  poisoned contract makes his withholding lethal — hers could become the same.

## Candidate 3: The Order / the Throng — mirror to the Promise itself

- Canon: infrastructure, not a cell; aware; patient; embedded in a city that
  doesn't see it
- **[PROPOSED]** shape: a bond network perverted — the Throng is a
  constellation with no individuals left in it. The dark mirror of a company
  held together by a dead woman's promise.

## Author Decision Needed

- Which candidate is the book's true mirror (others remain antagonists)?
- What is the mirror's Truth — the coherent philosophy that makes them right
  from inside? (None is attested yet.)
