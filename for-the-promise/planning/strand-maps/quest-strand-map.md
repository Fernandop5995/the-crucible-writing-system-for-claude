## The Burden

**What They Carry:** The Archwarden's summons, and then the contract it turns
out to conceal. The Council wants Johanna's Promise to escort researchers into
the deep forests of Vel'Era. Tauvor gives them enough of the horror to make the
stakes feel real and withholds enough of the truth to keep them from
understanding what they'd be walking into.

**The Poison in It:** The researchers are looking for a slumbering drake the
Council created the conditions for finding. The Promise delivered the Wyrshade.
They are being hired to walk into the consequence of their own delivery,
without being told what it is. Used twice for the same catastrophe.

**Why Them:** **[TBD]** — why the Council specifically wants the Promise (beyond
the delivery connection) is not stated. The full Thirteen traveled to Glintgate
and won't say why (open thread: The Archwarden's Silence).

## Movement I: Ignition — Glintgate (Chapters 1-6, written/mapped)

### The Arrival (Ch 1)
The Silver Road into Glintgate under the Archwarden's summons. Mikah at the
gate. A city that hates them and they hate back. Frederick already waiting
inside.

### The City Turns (Ch 2)
Followed since the gate. Damien's messenger at Bordou Square; insults,
accusations about Alessa's ambush, the Naaren spy counter-accusation. Damien
draws on Elaine; Brunor accepts the contest of arms.

### The Duel and Its Cost (Ch 4)
First blood to Damien; psychological warfare (Uthred, Elaine, Alessa); duel
becomes brawl; Elaine parts them, parrying both blades. Damien's parting words:
Golden Scale guildhall, by week's end. The rain argument; ends in silence.

### The Order Surfaces (Ch 5, first half)
The Scaleblade reveal: Celisia at the gate, the grey hood, the void where a
person should be. Charcoal on the handkerchief. The Order followed them into
Glintgate.

### The Council's Confession and Contract (Ch 5, second half)
The Wyrshade vivisection; fifteen researchers mad; the butchery; the voice in
an unknown tongue that means something *answered*. Then the pivot: the Vel'Era
escort contract. The trio neither accepts nor refuses.

### Three Problems at Once (Ch 6)
The Council's poisoned contract; Damien's political machinery through his wife
at the Golden Scale guildhall; Brunor's hunt reveals the Order's presence is
infrastructure — and it's aware it's being watched. The Glintgate arc has its
teeth in them.

  machinery goes quiet after the wife extracts what she wants (returns as a
  trap), or the Order goes still after Brunor's probing (returns having
  repositioned).

  II-III (departure, the deep forests, what the researchers actually find).
