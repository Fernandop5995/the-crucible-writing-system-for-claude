Before the merge, chroma and magic existed only in subtle and featureless ways. To the common folk, magic was merely a myth.
However, after a massive ball of chroma from space collided with the continent of Ao, it caused chroma to split the laws of nature into thirteen distinct forms, with some theorizing that a fourteenth form exists. In Eōtria, one cannot learn magic unless chosen by chroma, almost as if the world itself decides who will receive its power. Most mages are born already chosen, but many people awaken their magical abilities at a young age, with the likelihood of becoming a mage decreasing as they grow older. In rare cases, a mage may awaken to multiple schools of magic throughout their life. Those fortunate (or cursed, depending on who you ask) enough to experience this phenomenon are called Echowalkers. Some religions even teach that the world is alive and that the rifts appearing signify something far more grand than anyone realizes.
So the thirteen arcane pillars, otherwise known as the chromatic schools of magic are as follows. 
1. Restoration(Common rarity)
• Healing, regeneration, and recovery of body and spirit
• Most commonly used by battlefield healers and spiritual mages. The most powerful spells must be performed by using prayer and ceremony.
2. Transmutation(Uncommon rarity)
• Mages who engage with this school alter the physical properties of matter through spells or even alchemy
(metal to gold, wood to stone, etc.) Used moderately by most kingdoms Alchemy
• Magical chemistry — most transmuters practice alchemy. They transmute materials through ritual, potion, or chroma
• Practiced by both scientific and arcane communities
• Versatile, but dangerous when destabilized. Any object used in the transformation is forever imbued with the chroma of the caster. To cast the most powerful spells you must use a Transmuter’s Stone otherwise called by most, the alchemist’s stone. Regulated in most kingdoms for its potential economic and criminal use
3. Artifice/Runeweaving(Common rarity)
• Imbuing objects with magical function (runes, constructs, enchanted weapons).
Masters of invention, artificers use ingenuity and magic to unlock extraordinary capabilities in objects using glyphs and runes
• Artificers use a variety of tools to channel their arcane power. To cast a spell, an artificer might use an alchemist’s supplies to create a potent chromatic elixir, calligrapher's supplies to inscribe a rune of power into an item, or tinker's tools to craft a temporary glyph. One of the two discovered runes corresponds to this school and this school has been properly mastered by institutions all over Eōtria and Aetheryn.
4. Evocation(Common rarity)
• Manipulation of raw elemental forces (fire, lightning, frost, etc.)
• Powerful, aggressive, and often the most destructive form of magic. The most powerful spell must be used by chanting an incantation. Aerothurgy is the rarest and most difficult branch of Evocation to master. This branch is regulated heavily in most kingdoms for its military and criminal potential
5. Enchantment(Rare)
• Influencing minds, emotions, and behavior. Enchanters/Enchantresses from the school of enchantment affect the minds of others, influencing or controlling their behavior and even their perception with memories or knowledge
• Often feared and heavily regulated in most kingdoms for its potential political and criminal use
6. Illusion(Uncommon rarity)
• Creating false images, sounds, and perceptions. Some illusionists are harmless tricksters who use their spells to amuse. Others however are more sinister masters of deception, using their illusions to frighten and fool others for their gain.
• Useful for espionage, deception, and disorientation. Heavy regulations in most parts of the world for potential criminal and military use
7. Abjuration(Common rarity)
• Wards, shields, and magical protection, nullification of the other schools. Abjuration emphasizes spells that block, banish, and protect. Critics of this school say that its tradition is about denial, negation rather than positive assertion. Those chosen by this school however argue that ending harmful effects, protecting the weak, and banishing evil influences is anything but a philosophical void. It is a proud and respected school.
Called abjurers, members of this school are sought when baleful spirits require exorcism, when important locations must be guarded against magical spying, and when portals to other planes of existence must be closed.
• Kingdoms have learned to cast defensive wards around major cities or towns. Promoted and taught by schools and institutions the most worldwide alongside Artifice
8. Solumancy(Very rare) 
• Solumancy is an extremely rare dual-discipline involving the manipulation of light and shadow as opposing but interconnected forces. Only 1 in 10000 mages are picked to be solumancers. Those chosen by this school usually undergo a shift in personality as they can feel light and shadow mentally, physically, and even spiritually
• Solumancers argue that light and shadow are not elements, but forces of perception and truth meant to reveal or obscure the truth of something 
• Solumancers can cast sun or moon spells that can reveal or obscure, heal or harm, create vision or induce blindness. They also have mastery over light and dark
9. Divination(Rare)
• Diviners strive to part the veils of space, time, and consciousness so that one can see clearly. They work to master spells of discernment, remote viewing, supernatural knowledge, and foresight.
• Promoted and taught in certain kingdoms, outlawed in some parts of the world. Used by churches.
10. Conjuration(Uncommon)
• Those chosen by this pillar can focus on spells that conjure objects and creatures, and eventually with mastery transportation. Conjuration mages are strong utility wizards who can summon allies and conjure casting materials for spells. They can also learn transportation spells to teleport themselves across vast distances, even to other planes. 
• Includes teleportation, binding spirits, and planar manipulation
11. Necromancy(Rare)
• Those chosen by the pillar of Necromancy explore the cosmic forces of life, death, and undeath. Necromancers focus their studies in this tradition and learn to manipulate the energy that animates all living things. As they progress to mastery, they learn to sap the life force from a creature as magic destroys its body, transforming that vital energy into magical power they can manipulate.
Most people see necromancers as menacing, or even villainous, due to the close association with death. Not all necromancers are evil, but the forces they manipulate are considered taboo by many societies. Banned in all Eōtria but there are secluded practices of necromancy in the secluded workshops of Aethryn and certain shamans in Iskar.
12. **Kinumancy(Very Rare) (also referred to as Kymancy in Iskar)**
Kinumancy is a highly rare magical school that focuses on emotional resonance, the natural flow of living beings, instinctive movement, and the inner force that drives them. Practitioners are known as Kinumancers who possess the unique ability to interpret hidden intentions and sense residual emotions left behind by individuals in specific locations. Additionally, Kinumancers are capable of casting spells that enhance their movements as well as those of others.
Advanced Kinumancers can further channel their chroma through their bodies, achieving a state of perfect equilibrium. Taught in schools but heavily regulated for its potential political and criminal uses.
13. Chronurgy (Mythic)
• Those chosen by Chroma to become Chronomancers possess acute Temporal Awareness and can shift or manipulate time to alter the pace of reality to their liking. Using the ramping of anticipatory or spent chroma, masters of this school can bend the flow of time, lending themselves and their allies an advantage in the blink of an eye.

🌌 The Riftmarked
“Not born of legacy. Not forged by spell. Marked — as if by the world itself.”
⸻
🔹 Definition:
A Riftmarked is a person who bears a unique, living mark that appears before a rift manifests in the world — often weeks, sometimes months, in advance. This mark is not a brand, tattoo, or scar, but a mystical glyph embedded into their skin and soul. It pulses faintly with arcane light and grows more active the closer one is to the rift it foretells.
⸻
🔹 Known Traits:
• Only one person is Riftmarked per rift.
The mark is a precursor, not a result — the rift will appear within 100 leagues of where they were marked, regardless of where they go afterward.
• The mark reacts to emotion and stress, sometimes releasing uncontrolled magic (as we’ve seen with Elaine).
• Being Riftmarked amplifies the soul’s connection to one specific Rune School — even if the person has no formal magical training.
• The mark cannot be replicated, transferred, or removed — even by the most powerful mages.
⸻
🔹 Historical Theories:
• Historians and Runescribes believe the Riftmarked are keys, each one tethered to one of the thirteen runes lost across time.
• Religious sects (especially in Vel’Era and Halreth) view the Riftmarked as prophets or cursed vessels, depending on their ideology.
• The Caltheran Council and Naaren’s Blood Guild both view them as dangerous — and valuable. Many seek to control, recruit, or silence them before others do

8. CANON LOCKED — The Ashen Coast
Northeastern Naaren, Kingdom of Eòs

Geography:
The Ashen Coast occupies the far northeastern tip of Naaren where the kingdom’s forests and marshlands thin into open countryside and eventually give way to grey-stoned coastal bluffs above cold water. Caltheran presses in from the north and west as the dominant land border. To the east is open sea. To the south, Naaren proper stretches down through its forests and marshlands toward Keth’var Bastion, the granite-walled capital — a journey of several days through increasingly populated countryside.
It is a corner in the truest geographic sense. Two kingdoms meeting at its back, ocean at its face, and the rest of Naaren feeling like a different world entirely.
Character:
Technically Naaren. Practically ungoverned in any meaningful way. No guild presence, no mage institutions, no noble houses with holdings worth mentioning this far out. The kingdom’s administrative reach thins long before it arrives here. People on the Ashen Coast have more in common with Caltheran’s rural fringe population than with anything coming out of Keth’var. The border exists on maps. On the ground it’s a line nobody drew.
The shore itself is grey-stoned bluff country — not sand beach, but fine pale sediment ground from coastal rock over centuries that holds the color of cold ash in most light. The water is cold year-round. Mornings come in with salt fog off the sea. The fish are good. Strange things occasionally come up in nets.
The Village:
No formal name in common usage. Built into the ridge where the bluffs flatten enough to build on. A lamplighter route. A tavern by the lower road. Road workers, fishermen, people who arrived for reasons they’ve stopped explaining. Small enough that everyone knows Jackil’s name. Remote enough that a noble from Keth’var would have to be desperate to come here looking for anything.
Sea Traffic:
The eastern sea facing the Ashen Coast carries coastal shipping between Naaren’s northeastern ports and Caltheran’s shoreline where the two kingdoms meet the water. Occasionally longer routes. Renu watches it from the dock without particular interest.
Position in Naaren:
Keth’var Bastion and the capital infrastructure are far south. The Shadowfen marshes — Naaren’s most politically significant geographic feature — are southwest, near the Caltheran border on that side. The Ashen Coast sits at the opposite end of the kingdom from everything that matters to Naaren’s power structure.
Narrative significance:
Slovyn Yldritch, of House Yldritch, Naaren noble, searched from the northwest reaches of Varkaan across Caltheran, Halreth, and Vel’Era before being directed back to the northeastern tip of her own kingdom. The place she could theoretically have checked before leaving. The last name on the list. A fishing village at the edge of Naaren that Naaren itself has largely forgotten.
Renu being here is already slightly strange. The creature in the bucket is slightly strange. That the search ended here, in Naaren’s own forgotten corner, is the shape of something no one has named yet.
Notable residents: Jackil, Renu, Varney, Hance

The Throng is a cosmic, hive-mind entity originating from Ubil. They are a superior lifeform akin to an eldritch horror.
 * Goal: The Throng does not conquer; it infects and assimilates. It consumes all life—flora, fauna, and people—and repurposes the biomass to expand its hive.
 * Method: The assimilation is a brutal, body-horror process, transforming victims into grotesque new additions to the swarm.
 * Intelligence: They are not mindless. They operate with a collective, hive-like intelligence and a clear command structure.
 * Mechanics: The Throng cannot assimilate a living person being shielded by Abjuration magic. However, the moment a person is killed, their body can be turned.
#### The Rune-Drakes: Corrupted Guardians
The fourteen Rune-Drakes were created by the goddess Eōtria to be the world's protectors. They absorbed the cataclysmic energy of The Merge and entered a deep slumber, acting as immense reservoirs of pure magic.
 * The Corruption Process: The Throng's primary objective is to find these slumbering Guardians via psychic or physical connection. The Throng do not know the location of the drakes.
Once found, the Throng’s essence will slowly infect and assimilate a Drake with their blight over a period of days, months, or even years.
 * The Awakening: Once the assimilation reaches 100%, the Drake is forcefully awakened as a fully corrupted Commander of the Throng and travels to the rift it is connected to. It then uses its immense power of the corresponding pillar to open its Rift fully and summon the main swarm to bring about the end of humanity. 
#### The Rifts and the Runes
The Rifts are portals to the Throng's dimension in space time . Each of the thirteen Rifts and runes corresponds to a pillar of magic and a slumbering Drake.

 * The Runes: When a rift appears and a drake is threatened, Eōtria herself creates a Rune inside with a will of its own. This Rune links a chosen individual—the Riftmarked—to the pure, uncorrupted power of the slumbering Drake.

The Riftmarked are the key to defeating the Throng. They are chosen by the Runes created by Eōtria.

Seat of the High King of Eòs from the Halvyr royal line. The five kingdoms — Varkaan, Caltheran, Naaren, Halreth, Vel’Era — retain their internal governance, cultural identity, and ruling houses but answer upward to the High King. They are sovereign in character, vassal in practice.
Military:
Caer Seryth remains the military heart of the city — the original fortress now embedded in the urban sprawl of Johanna’s Promise like a fist inside a glove. The city has grown around it but the fortress still runs it in the ways that matter.
Academic:
Home to the most prestigious arcane academy on the continent, founded by Lune during the close of the Fifth Era. By 1142 A.M. it has been operational for over a century and has produced generations of the continent’s most powerful mages.
In 1142 A.M. — Age of Peace:
The city is at its most prosperous and its most politically layered. Two centuries of peace means the idealism that built Johanna’s Promise has largely calcified into institution. The people who remember what the war cost are dead. What remains is comfort, ambition, old grievances dressed in new clothes, and a city large enough that things can disappear inside it — people, money, inheritance, wives.
Exactly the kind of place a desperate noble would be chasing a lost fortune. Exactly the kind of place a missing person might end up. Exactly the kind of place a seventeen year old from the edge of the world has never seen and is about to.

# ### Part 5: The Grand Narrative Arc
#### The Two-Series Saga
The story is divided into two five-"book" series:
 * Eōtria (The Prequel): The current story. This series will chronicle the 50-year war against the Threat Level 4 Throng invasion that emerges from the Kymancy Rift. It will conclude with the closing of the Rift and the founding of Brunor's unified kingdom.
 * Solsyrr (The Main Story): Takes place 203 years into the Sixth Era. It will follow the descendants of the original heroes.
#### The Main Story Protagonists
 * Jenova Halvyr: Brunor's great-great-granddaughter and the heir to the unified kingdom. She is rumored to be the spitting image of Johanna Halvyr and will be the Riftmarked of Chronurgy (Time).
 * Wilhiem: The Hollow's great-great-grandson. He is the mortal heir to the divinely-blessed "true blood" lineage and will be the Riftmarked of the Spiral Pillar (Creation).
#### The Endgame
 * The Prequel Goal: To survive the 50-year war, close the Kymancy Rift, and unify the kingdoms.
 * The Main Story Goal: The thirteen Runes gathered over the generations must be combined to forge the Rune of Creation. Wielding this, Wilhiem must travel into the final Rift connecting Eōtria to the sun to confront the corrupted sun god, Solsyrr, and end the threat of the Throng forever.
#### Core Themes
The saga is built on grounded, philosophical, and human themes:
 * The Fight to Stay Human: The central theme. Characters battle external corruption (the Throng) and internal erosion from trauma, duty, and immortality.
 * Legacy, Fate, and Found Family: The weight of the past, the unbreakable bonds of destiny, and the strength of relationships forged in hardship.
 * Order vs. Chaos: The struggle between the structured world of humanity and the alien, assimilating chaos of the Throng.

# The Houses of the Academy
The academy would be divided into houses, each representing one of the five original vassal kingdoms. Students would be sorted based on their magical affinity, their lineage, or their family's political allegiance. This structure would not only foster competition but also serve as a microcosm of the political maneuvering that still exists between the kingdoms.
The Five Factions of the Eos National Arcane Academy
Students at the academy are sorted into one of five factions, each named in honor of a legendary hero of the Fifth Era.
• The White Bears: Named after Uthred Halvyr, the founder of Johanna's Promise and a legendary warrior. This faction represents the ideals of martial prowess, discipline, and strength.
• The Raven's Watch: Named after Elaine Morrandis, the former scout commander and Riftmarked of Kymancy. This faction represents the ideals of spiritualism, emotional control, and tactical cunning.
• Hollow Hearts: Named after The Hollow, the powerful mage of legend of the mythic Spiral Pillar. This faction represents the ideals of potential, mystery, and spirit.
• The Black Dogs: Named after The First High King Brunor, the king of the unified kingdoms. This faction represents the ideals of loyalty, authority, and command.
• The Silver Bulls: Named after Frederick Rowain, the moral anchor of the original company. This faction represents the ideals of endurance, resilience, and the strength of a quiet, unshakable heart, soul, and morality.
The Academy's Location
The Eos National Arcane Academy is located near the keep of Caer Seryth. Over the centuries, the growing academy has merged with the keep, physically and metaphorically uniting the old world of military might with the new world of arcane knowledge. This is a powerful visual of the original company's legacy becoming the foundation of a new era.
