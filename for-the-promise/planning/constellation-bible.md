## Johanna's Promise (the company)

Founded by Uthred Halvyr after Johanna's death — "a mercenary company built on
restraint, purpose, and redemption." Creed: "We fight to protect." Home keep:
Caer Seryth, six hundred souls. Chain of command: Chief (Uthred) → Commanders →
Lieutenants → First Captains → Captains → Squad Leaders → Veterans → Recruits.
Four divisions: Garrison (Brunor), Scouts (Elaine), Quartermasters (Frederick),
Healers (West). Sigil: silver flame; Johanna's pendant (sword and earth) is the
company's badge of moral responsibility. Her final words — "I wish to see a
paradise for all men." — are carved into the war table.

Book 2 state: the Promise suffered "the siege" — the Order attacked; Damien:
"To lose the rift and your dignity in the same night" (Ch 2). Alessa was
ambushed two months before Book 2 opens. The company is summoned before the
Caltheran Council.

## The Trio

### Brunor Halvyr — Garrison Commander, POV (Ch 1, 4)
- 27 (Book 1 sheets); "The Black Blade"; born Brunor Halvyr VI; son of Uthred
  and the late Johanna Halvyr; secretly descended from Caltheran royalty
- 6'0", broad, dense muscle; dark brownish-black hair; steel-grey eyes; light
  olive complexion
- Wields **Nullbrand** (the Black Blade): unknown metallurgy, believed to
  predate the rise of Chroma; passively cancels all magic near it; hums when
  something magical resists it. Mutes Elaine's mark in proximity.
- Elite swordsman (trained by Uthred); defense-first, hunts the flaw in the
  opponent's pattern; more skilled than Elaine or Frederick with a blade
- Headaches when memories run long (Ch 1) — recurring condition
- Haunted by: Johanna's death; the Stonefield inn; the execution of Ephram (a
  teenage deserter hanged under his command); "the dead boy"
- **Merea**: secret romantic relationship (sheets); he told Elaine and
  Frederick "about Merea" in her quarters — Elaine congratulated him wearing
  "despair covered by that emotionless mask" (Ch 4). Phoebe discovered the
  affair (sheets) and dislikes that Elaine's love goes unseen.
- Unaware Elaine loves him (sheets, Book 1 sample)
- Order foil: **Judas Wynvale, "The Shepherd"** — passive null vs striking null
- Quote: "It's not courage if you're not afraid... Lead with your spine, not
  your voice."

### Elaine Morrandis — 5th Scout Commander, POV (Ch 2, 5)
- 23 (Book 1 sheets); "The Dancer (of Steel)", "The Raven", "El"
- One of the two last living heirs of House Morrandis of Vel'Era (cadet branch
  of House Arsenia, the ruling family); blood-related to High Queen Lysandria
  Arsenia; house destroyed in a political purge — mother Juna, father
  Hunbuvial, brother Hervan all dead. Rescued by Uthred and Brunor from her
  burning home; raised in the Promise; trained chiefly by Uthred and Tella.
- Damien Beauforth is her cousin; he brands her "the Bastard," says she
  "disgraced my house with the smear of your birth" and "should have been left
  in Qunthren bay the night they pulled you from your mother"; assassins tried
  and failed to kill her as an infant (Ch 2). **[TBD: exact parentage — heir
  and "bastard" both attested]**
- 5'8", lean, dancer's build; jet-black shoulder-length hair; pale
  steel-grey-blue eyes; missing her left pinky (stub aches under stress);
  raven-feathered cloak (three feathers at the pin among hundreds); black
  sword, white leather grip, purple amethyst in ivory hilt; matched black
  horse, **Munch** (gift from Brunor at her captain promotion)
- **The riftmark** (Kymancy): left arm/hand. Reads emotion, intent, presence;
  opens at thresholds; floods in crowds; detects voids (Scaleblades). Puts
  weight behind every word she speaks. "Potentially the most dangerous woman
  on the continent and a political disaster for the Vel'Eran queen should she
  choose to be one" — Tella (Ch 3). "Dangerous to her enemies and to herself."
- Awakening history (Book 1): flare after the War Chamber; restrained in the
  isolation ward; chromatic suppressant; West's warning — push it untrained
  and she hollows out. A second heartbeat pulsing from the East, the
  Shadowfen: "I see you, little vessel."
- In love with Brunor — unsaid (sheets; Book 1 sample); congratulated him
  about Merea behind a mask (Ch 4)
- Gives copper carp to beggars until stopped (Ch 2); wanted the amethyst
  necklace Hans bought to taunt her
- Order foil: **Caelus, "The Silencer"** — Master Kymancer; her connection vs
  his isolation

### Frederick Rowain — 7th Supply Commander (Quartermaster), POV (Ch 6 planned)

## Continuity Flags (for the author — found while reconciling sources)

1. **Telvor vs Tauvor — RESOLVED by compendium.** Canon is **Archwarden Rhen
   Tauvor**. Ch 1's "the mark of Archwarden Telvor" needs a one-word fix.
2. **Twelve vs Thirteen — RESOLVED by compendium.** Canon is the **Council of
   Twelve** (eleven Wardens + the Archwarden). The roadmap's "full Thirteen"
   is the outlier. (Note the resonance available anyway: the Order fields ten
   of thirteen drake-helms — the Council of Twelve vs the Order's thirteen is
   a deliberate-looking asymmetry if the author wants it.)
3. **Council timing.** Roadmap Beat 1: "Timeskip — one week after the tavern."
   Prose: Frederick says "the council tomorrow" (Ch 1) and the College scene
   happens the day after the duel ("the evening before", Ch 5). The prose
   timeline is: 7th campfire → 8th arrival/duel/tavern → 9th council.
4. **Golden Scale vs Golden Scales** guildhall (Ch 2 vs Ch 4).
5. **Erasmus (Ch 1 prose) vs Esmarus IV (sheets).**
6. **Lysandra (Ch 1 prose) vs Lysandria (sheets).**
7. **Revanius vs Revianus** (sheets use both spellings for Mark).
8. **Aerra / Aeraa / Serah** — the Naaren double agent's name appears three
   ways in the sheets.
9. **Caer Seryth's realm — RESOLVED by Director's Notes.** The Vespur Plains
   belong to Halreth; the keep sits at their eastern narrowing at the base of
   Vespine's Cradle, and the Cradle's north side is Caltheran. Both datelines
   can be right — but verify Ch 1's "half a league to the south," which reads
   short for crossing the range.
10. **"Mark" the person vs "the mark."** Ch 5 uses "Off Mark" for the person
    while "the mark" names Elaine's riftmark in the same scene — easy for
    readers to trip over; consider a surname or epithet in that line.
11. **Elaine: heir and bastard.** Sheets make her a last heir of House
    Morrandis; Damien calls her bastard-born. Both can be true — but the
    mechanics (whose bastard, why Beauforth claims her as "my house") are
    unstated.
12. **Order of the WRYM** (heading typo in sheets) — the body text uses Wyrm.
13. **Book 2's year.** Manuscript datelines: **982 A.M.** Compendium timeline:
    Book 1 *Riftmarked* = 981-982; Book 2 *For the Promise* = **983-985**.
    Reconcile before more datelines are written.
14. **Johanna's final words.** Sheets: "I wish to see a paradise for all men."
    Compendium: "I wish of a paradise for all men." Pick one (it's carved into
    the war table).
15. **Morninghall.** Queen Unara Lackmin and the Knights of Piety (Ch 5 prose)
    do not appear in the compendium's rulers/settlements — new Book 2 canon to
    backfill into the source docs.
