BOOK 2: FOR THE PROMISE
Draft Roadmap
8th of Ashenfall, 982 A.M. — Late Summer, Month of Death (The Stranger)

# Chapters Written
## Chapter 1 — Brunor POV
The Arrival
The campfire in the Blackwood Forest. Brunor and Elaine on the road before Glintgate. The clearing, the rabbits, the two moons. The flashback to the watchtower in Hudria — Johanna’s words about the stone, about legacy, about what it means to carry a name. The next morning, the Silver Road into Glintgate. Mikah at the gate. The city through Brunor’s eyes — a place that hates them and they hate back. Frederick already waiting inside. Establishes the Archwarden’s summons as the reason they’re here.
## Chapter 2 — Elaine POV
The Square
The city turns hostile fast. The necklace, the man in the alley — they’ve been followed since the gate. Damien’s messenger at Bordou Square. Brunor puts a dagger to the man’s throat. Damien arrives with his guards. Insults, accusations about Alessa’s ambush, the Naaren spy counter-accusation. Damien draws on Elaine. Brunor accepts the contest of arms. Ends with the duel beginning.
## Chapter 3 — Phoebe POV
The Keep
Caer Seryth. Phoebe training in the yard, struggling with her sequence. Tella arrives and corrects her — mentor and student, rough warmth and hard truths. Phoebe worries about Elaine walking into Damien’s city with Brunor’s restlessness and Frederick barely cleared by West. Tella reminds her who trained Elaine. Who taught her to use words like daggers and swords like bargaining chips. The riftmark puts weight behind every word she speaks. Damien is afraid of her. The chapter closes on Phoebe walking away, limping on a blister, whispering the truth Tella didn’t say: dangerous to her enemies and to herself.
## Chapter 4 — Brunor POV
The Duel
Bordou Square in the rain. Brunor reads Damien’s footwork — court fencing built on a spearman’s instincts. First blood to Damien. The psychological warfare begins: Uthred, Elaine, then Alessa. Brunor charges. The duel turns into a brawl. Elaine steps between them, parrying both blades simultaneously — Nullbrand in one hand, the golden blade in the other. Damien’s parting words: Golden Scale guildhall, by week’s end. Then the rain scene. Elaine’s fury, Brunor’s stubbornness, the argument about Alessa and what he kept from her. Ends in silence.
## Chapter 5 — Elaine POV (First Half Written)
The Tavern
The inn. Frederick, Brunor, Elaine at the table. The mark flooding Elaine with the room’s emotional noise — the farmhands by the hearth, the merchant counting coins, the woman watching the stairs. Beatrice the serving girl eyeing Frederick. The confrontation about Alessa — Frederick admits he knew, refuses to let Brunor take the blame alone. “Two ropes. I’d rather neither, but I’m a quartermaster. I count what’s there.” The Scaleblade reveal — Celisia at the gate, the grey hood, the void where a person should be. Charcoal on the handkerchief. The chapter’s pivot: the Order followed them into Glintgate.

# Chapter 5 — Second Half (To Draft)
The Council
### Beat 1 — The Chamber
Timeskip — one week after the tavern. The day of the Council meeting. The three of them walk into a chamber the Council has secured somewhere in Glintgate. Not their sanctum, not the Archive, not Aedric Spire. The full Thirteen are present — all in identical Caltheran robes, seated, projecting unity. Nobody explains why they traveled here. Nobody offers a reason. The trio notices. Frederick and Brunor exchange a look.
Elaine’s mark opens the moment she crosses the threshold. Every fracture in the room starts bleeding through the performance — fear off some of the Wardens, resentment off others, guilt buried deep in at least one. Tauvor sits at the center. Pale, sickly, muscular, black hair to his neck. His voice fills the room the way it always does — measured and purposeful. He owns the space without raising it.
### Beat 2 — The Confession
Tauvor opens with the Wyrshade. Careful about it — laying the groundwork like a man building a bridge he knows is rotten. The vivisection. The illusion it cast. Fifteen of their finest researchers and scholars hit with visions none of them were built to survive. The Rift. The rune. What waits inside. Every single one of them went mad from the assault.
Elaine listens, and she recognizes what he’s describing before he names it. Kymancy. Detect Memory — a spell that lets the caster tap into the history of a place and see it. The Wyrshade didn’t just attack them. It showed them something and made them look. She keeps this recognition to herself.
The mark reads Tauvor throughout — genuine fear beneath the composure. This is a man confessing a disaster he caused and still trying to control the narrative around it. The other Wardens react unevenly. The mark catches which ones already knew the full account and which are hearing details for the first time. Not all Thirteen were told equally.
### Beat 3 — The Butchery
After the visions took hold, the researchers turned on each other. Absolute savagery — wounds that should have been fatal meant nothing. They kept tearing into one another, kept moving, kept going, until mages were brought in to destroy them with fire. Even Tauvor’s composure thins here. The mark reads it — this is not a man performing fear. This is a man who saw this happen and hasn’t slept right since.
Elaine processes this through the lens of what she knows about the Throng from Melody’s Book 1 intelligence and the Scaleblades. The researchers weren’t just driven mad — they were connected to something. The hive mind. The Council doesn’t have a name for what happened. Elaine does, and she can’t say it.
Brunor goes still the way he does when he’s processing something that changes the shape of a war. Frederick is doing the arithmetic behind his eyes — counting what this costs, what it means, where the weight falls.
### Beat 4 — The Voice
The final detail. One of the researchers, body already destroyed, fire eating through him, spoke. Not his voice. Something else used his mouth and said words in a language nobody in the room recognized before the flames took his throat. Tauvor provides the words — transcribed phonetically by the mages who witnessed it. Untranslated. Unidentified.
The room changes after this. The voice is what frightens the Archwarden more than the dead. Because the voice means something heard them. Something answered.
### Beat 5 — The Pivot
The trio asks what the voice said. Nobody knows. This pulls the conversation into First Era history — the Council’s scholars reaching back into the oldest records the Archive holds, trying to identify the tongue, trying to understand what spoke through a burning man. The records suggest the voice may be connected to something old that sleeps in the deep forests of Vel’Era.
### Beat 6 — The Contract
And this is where Tauvor pivots. Because the real reason they’re here lands now. The Council wants Johanna’s Promise to escort and guard a team of researchers into the deep forests of Vel’Era. Standard protection work on the surface — exactly the kind of job the Promise takes. He doesn’t tell them about the drake. He doesn’t tell them what the researchers are actually looking for. He gives them enough of the horror to make them understand the stakes are real, and withholds enough of the truth to keep them from understanding what they’d actually be walking into.
The chapter closes on the trio’s response. They don’t accept on the spot. They don’t refuse. They leave the chamber carrying more questions than they walked in with, and a contract offer that smells wrong but pays well, from people who just confessed to getting fifteen of their own slaughtered by something they still don’t understand. Elaine knows more than she said. Brunor knows something about this is off. Frederick is already counting the cost of saying yes and the cost of saying no.

# Chapter 6 — Frederick POV (To Draft)
The Golden Scale and the Hunt
Two threads running simultaneously. Frederick is the lens for both, even when he’s not present for one of them.
### Thread One — The Guildhall
Frederick and Elaine arrive at the Golden Scale guildhall for the meeting with Damien. Brunor is supposed to be with them. He isn’t. He was meant to attend. Instead, after whatever the Council meeting shook loose, he’s gone into the city following his own leads — connections from the old days, people who owe him, contacts who trade in the kind of information that doesn’t get written down. He didn’t tell Frederick where he was going. He told Elaine, barely, and she let him go because she needed someone pulling that thread and she couldn’t do it herself while sitting across from Damien’s wife.
Because it’s not Damien at the guildhall. It’s his wife. Damien is elsewhere — either making a point by not showing, or letting his wife handle the diplomacy the way she apparently does when the situation calls for precision instead of a golden blade. Frederick reads this the moment he walks in. The wife is a different kind of danger than Damien. Measured where he’s volatile. Gracious where he’s cruel. The kind of person who offers you exactly what you need and watches your face while you decide whether the price is hidden in the offer. Elaine’s mark is working the room — reading the wife’s real emotional state beneath whatever she’s performing, reading the guards, reading the servants, reading the walls of a guildhall that belongs to a man who wants them dead or useful and hasn’t decided which.
Frederick holds this meeting together because that’s what he does. He’s the wall behind Brunor’s spine and the ground beneath Elaine’s feet, and today he’s both at once. The conversation with the wife is political — whatever Damien wants from the Promise, she’s laying the groundwork for it. Frederick’s job is to listen, count what’s real, and keep Elaine from saying anything that can’t be taken back.
### Thread Two — The Hunt
Meanwhile, Brunor is in the city. Frederick doesn’t see this directly — he learns it after, or in fragments. Brunor is pulling on threads that came out of the Council meeting. The Order’s presence in Glintgate. Who they’ve touched. What they’ve built. The kind of rot that looks like ordinary corruption until the pattern sharpens into something deliberate. He follows connections. He finds things that don’t add up — a name that shouldn’t be known, a transaction that doesn’t match the goods, a silence where there should be noise.
The investigation turns from one-directional to something else. The thing he’s studying has been studying him back. The Order knows they’re being looked into. The rot runs deep enough that even poking at it draws attention.
### Convergence
The chapter closes with the two threads converging. Frederick and Elaine leave the guildhall. Brunor finds them, or they find him. What he’s carrying changes the shape of everything — not just the Order’s presence in Glintgate, but the scale of it. This isn’t a cell or a scout. This is infrastructure. And it’s aware.
The three of them are now sitting on three problems at once: the Council’s poisoned contract, Damien’s political machinery through his wife, and the Order watching them watch it. The Glintgate arc has its teeth in them.

# Open Threads
The Archwarden’s Silence. The full Thirteen traveled to Glintgate and won’t say why. That question hangs unanswered. It could be because whatever happened in the Archive made Aedric Spire unsafe. It could be because Tauvor wants this conversation far from the sanctum. It could be related to the Order’s influence. Nobody gets an answer.
Celisia’s Deliberate Reveal. She wanted Elaine to know she was there. The why hasn’t been answered yet. Seed it, don’t resolve it.
Brunor’s Silence. He still hasn’t said the thing Elaine wanted him to say. Frederick covered for him at the tavern. That debt is still open.
Damien’s Blindness. He thinks the war is political. He doesn’t know what’s standing in his city. Whether that ignorance protects him or dooms him is a question the Glintgate arc needs to begin answering.
Alessa. The ambush, the accusation, Damien’s counter-accusation about a Naaren spy. This thread is live and unresolved. Brunor’s investigation in chapter 6 could pull on it.
Caelus. Elaine’s direct Scaleblade foil. Not yet confirmed in Glintgate, but the possibility that the void she felt was his influence rather than Celisia’s nature is worth planting.
The Poisoned Contract. The Council is sending the Promise to guard researchers looking for a slumbering drake they created the conditions for finding. The Promise delivered the Wyrshade. Now the Council is hiring them to walk into the consequence of that delivery without telling them what it is. They’re being used twice for the same catastrophe.

# POV Rotation
Brunor → Elaine → Phoebe → Brunor → Elaine → Frederick
The two Brunor chapters bracket the duel from setup to aftermath. Phoebe gets the home front interlude. Elaine carries the political and supernatural weight across two chapters. Frederick takes the lens for the investigation and the Damien meeting, giving him agency outside the Brunor-Elaine conflict for the first time.
