• Tellen Langral Oravalie: Garrison: Recruit
A lanky, insecure boy from a northern Varkaan fishing village, Tellen joined Johanna’s Promise to escape the life of a fisherman and chase his dreams of becoming a hero. Though often clumsy and mocked for his unrefined background, he possesses a surprising, raw instinct for swordplay that emerges in moments of high pressure. He is driven by a deep desire to prove his worth and harbors a youthful crush on Captain Phoebe Morrandis, whose presence often triggers his latent potential.  
• Mara: Quartermasters: Recruit
A cynical and pragmatic recruit from the slums of a unnamed small town in Caltheran, Mara is Tellen's sharp-tongued opposite. She views the heroic legends of the Promise with disdain, believing that mercenary work is about survival and coin, not honor. She was publicly humbled in the training yard by Captain Phoebe Morrandis, whose superior skill and intellect forced Mara into a state of resentful respect.  Mara is nineteen years old and despite her usually sharp and confident attitude, she is prideful and worries about not being good enough to herself. She has dark blackish brown hair that drops down to her waist, usually tied into a ponytail. Her eyes are a deep seated hazelnut blue. She is a skilled but dirty fighter.
• Juno: Healers: Recruit
A quiet, observant young man with dark skin, brown soft eyes and a strong face, Juno hails from a small fishing village on the southern coast that was wiped out by magically-infused tidal waves during a freak storm. He is one of the few survivors, having lost his family and home in a single night. He joined Johanna’s Promise not for glory or wealth, but out of sheer necessity, seeking a new purpose and a place to belong. His reserved nature hides a deep resilience and a simmering desire to gain the strength necessary to protect others from the kind of tragedy that shattered his life.
• Rynn: Scouts: Recruit
The older of the twins by a mere ten minutes, Rynn is the pragmatic and protective half of the pair. Growing up on the war-torn borderlands between Nareen and Caltheran, she saw firsthand that glory is often just a pretty word for a shallow grave. She is a capable brawler with a sharp, calculating mind, quick wit yet humble disposition. Her brown wavy hair drops to her chin and part of it is styled back to revealed her entire face. She has dark blue eyes and a oval shaped face. Rynn is twenty one.
Rynn joined the Promise for one reason only: to keep her idealistic brother, Rinn, alive. She sees the company as a means to a steady wage and a relatively safe place for him to get his heroic notions out of his system.
• Rinn: Scouts: Recruit
The younger twin, Rinn, is a dreamer who grew up on the legends of Uthred Halvyr and the noble ideals of Johanna’s Promise. He sees the world not as it is, but as it should be. It was his unwavering belief in heroism that convinced a reluctant Rynn to join the company with him. He is cheerful, charismatic, and perhaps a bit naive, eager to prove himself a hero. Rinn has brown short hair with dark blue eyes with an oval shaped face. He is fiercely loyal to his sister, though he remains blissfully unaware that her primary motivation is simply to keep him from getting killed.

Status: Alive, active command
Location: Caer Seryth

### Appearance:
Hair: Dark brownish-black, short and practical, often left unstyled; a few strands fall across his brow.
Eyes: Deep, muted grayish-steel; difficult to read, heavy with calculation and memory.
Skin: Light olive complexion, weathered by sun and exposure, with faint scarring across arms and torso from years in the field.
Build: Broad-shouldered, dense muscle; strong but not bulky. Carries himself with control rather than aggression.
Height: Approx. 6’0” (183 cm)
Style: Always in uniform — dark layered tunics or field gear, marked with the commander’s sigil. Never seen unarmed. Wears his blade with reverence, as though it is both shield and burden. Casual wear is usually dark tunic and leathers.
Expression: Quiet intensity. Rarely emotive; his silences speak more than his words. His gaze is often lowered in thought or held still, scanning.
To many, Brunor is the archetype of a disciplined commander — not charismatic in the traditional sense, but respected for the depth of his resolve. A still presence in chaos. He leads from the front, not by grand gestures but by action and weight.

### Core Traits:
Disciplined, introspective, morally grounded, emotionally guarded, shaped by loss
Strengths: Elite swordsmanship, high-pressure clarity, battlefield tactics, unshakable restraint, trust-building among those closest to him, loyal and trustworthy
Flaws: Emotionally closed off, hesitant to trust his own instincts in matters of the arcane, slow to forgive himself, burdens others’ mistakes as his own
Current Arc: Brunor faces the encroaching unknown —, the arcane, and the growing instability in the world beyond steel. As the mystical threatens the rational, he must grapple with whether his leadership is enough — and whether the legacy he’s inherited is one he should preserve or transform.

### Family & Background:
Family: Son of Uthred Halvyr and Johanna Halvyr. Both legends in their own right — one still breathing, the other martyred.
Lineage: Secretly descended from Caltheran royalty through the Halvyr line. He does not claim this title, but its political weight looms.
Found by / Trained by / Mentors: Trained by his father Uthred in both sword and leadership. Johanna shaped his ideals. Now shaped equally by the counsel of Frederick, the presence of Elaine, and the specter of his own past.
Motivation: To lead with purpose, to protect what Johanna’s Promise stands for, and to ensure no one under his command bears the weight of mercy or failure alone — as he did with Ephram.
Background:
Born as Brunor Halvyr VI, he is the son of Uthred Halvyr and the late Johanna Halvyr, two legendary figures tied to both the Varkaan and Caltheran legacy. Raised within a military settlement in northern Caltheran during a time of a long-standing war between Varkaan and Caltheran, Brunor inherited a complex legacy — one shaped by duty, theology, and war. His father Uthred Halvyr taught him early on how to command the blade and flag, now a stoic but cautious man.
The Halvyr family grew disillusioned with the kingdom’s scorched-earth approach which resulted in his father defecting from Varkaan when Brunor was young. Uthred eventually forged Johanna’s Promise after Johanna’s death, a mercenary company built on restraint, purpose, and redemption after Johanna died under mysterious circumstances. Brunor was among its first captains and eventually became a commander through hard work.

## **THE TEN LEADERS OF THE ORDER OF THE WYRM**

### **Final Draft — Canon Lock**

-----

NAME: THE SCALEBLADES

*“They wear the faces of the world’s guardians as a mockery — and as a promise.”*

All ten champions wear variants of the same distinctive armor: **Ebony Plate** — heavy black armor forged from an alloy the Order calls *Voidsteel*, a metal that drinks light and seems to shift in shadow. Made with artifice and alchemy. The armor is standardized in design philosophy but adapted to each warrior’s combat style:

- **Heavy variants** feature full plate with articulated joints and thick pauldrons
- **Medium variants** reduce bulk at the limbs while maintaining core protection
- **Agile variants** strip to half-plate or brigandine construction, prioritizing mobility while retaining the distinctive black aesthetic

The armor’s surface bears no insignia, no heraldry, no identification — only the faint, almost imperceptible etchings of *dead runes* along the seams, symbols that once held power but now serve only as decoration. Or so it appears.

**The Helmets:**
Each champion wears a unique closed helm, and upon each is engraved the stylized visage of one of the **Thirteen Rune-Drakes**. The engravings are subtle — worked into the brow, the cheek guards, the breathing slits — but unmistakable to those who know the old legends.

This detail is deeply unsettling. There are only ten champions present. The helmets suggest there were meant to be **thirteen**.

*Three are missing. Three have already fallen — or have yet to arrive.*

### #### **1. JUDAS WYNVALE — “The Shepherd”**

**Role:** Head of the Order’s Combat Cell  
**Class:** Master Abjurer  
**Weapon:** *“The Blade of Severance"* — A slender, pale steel longsword  
**Helmet Engraving:** **Vael’Thamar, the Aegis-Wyrm** (Abjuration Drake)

-----

**Armor:**
Judas wears the heaviest variant of the Ebony Plate — full coverage from gorget to sabatons, with a high collar that frames his helm. The armor is immaculate, as if it has never seen battle, though the pale steel of his blade tells a different story. His gauntlets are reinforced for parrying, and the joints are articulated with silent precision. He moves without the usual clatter of heavy plate.

**Helmet:**
A closed great helm with a smooth, featureless faceplate broken only by a narrow horizontal visor slit. The engraving of **Vael’Thamar** coils across the brow and down the cheeks — silver scales rendered in delicate relief, horns like coiled wards framing the temples. The effect is that of a guardian watching from within.

**Combat Philosophy:**
Judas represents the dark inversion of Abjuration’s ideals. His blade, The Hush, is a **Striking Null** — it dissolves magic on contact. Unlike Brunor’s Black Blade (which creates a passive field), The Hush is surgical. If Judas parries a fireball, it vanishes. If he cuts a ward, it shatters. If he strikes a Riftmark, the bearer *screams*.

His style is **minimalist and clinical**. He fights with the patience of a man who has already won. He waits for opponents to rely on their power, then deletes it and strikes the opening. He is what happens when protection becomes *denial*.

**Appearance beneath the helm:**
Aristocratic, sandy hair going grey at the temples. Pale blue eyes, almost colorless. He looks like a disappointed father rather than a warlord. Slender and tall.

**Foil:** Brunor Halvyr

#### **2. CELISIA YLMINDIAN — “The Witch”**

**Role:** Second-in-Command / Siege Specialist/ Transporter  
**Class:** Master Conjurer  
**Weapons:** *“Kingmaker”* (Macestaff) and (Tower Shield)  
**Helmet Engraving:** **Qereth, the Spiral Gate** (Conjuration Drake)

**Armor:**
Celisia wears a heavy variant modified for her unique combat style — full plate with reinforced pauldrons and a lower back brace designed to anchor her against the recoil of her own magic. Her armor is the most battle-scarred of the ten, dented and gouged from impacts that would have killed lesser warriors. She does not repair the damage. Each mark is a lesson.

**Helmet:**
A closed barbute-style helm with a T-shaped opening that reveals nothing but shadow within. The engraving of **Qereth** seems to flicker across the metal — wings that shimmer with starlight, a form that appears to phase in and out of existence depending on the angle of view. The effect is disorienting. Looking at her helm too long induces vertigo.

**Combat Philosophy:**
Celisia is a **Spellsword-Tank** — a Conjurer who fights in full plate at close range. She does not summon armies. She weaponizes the *spaces between things*.

- **The Flail (“The Passage”):** When she swings, she opens a micro-portal at the flail’s head and another behind her enemy. The weapon phases through space, striking from impossible angles without her ever moving.

She rarely speaks in battle, yet her voice is smooth and careful. Prefers to stay back and provide support unless close combat is needed. 

**Appearance beneath the helm:**
Late twenties, hawkish face, long greyish-hair that is almost silver. Her hazel green eyes are calculating, constantly measuring angles and distances. Her voice is soft and careful. 

**Foil:** Lune

#### **3. BRYCEN — “The Horrower”**

**Role:** Third / The Experiment / Terror Weapon  
**Class:** Echowalker (Three Pillars: Artifice, Transmutation, Necromancy)  
**Weapons:** His own body  
**Helmet Engraving:** **Dethros, the Hallowed Light** (Necromancy Drake)

-----

**Armor:**
Brycen’s armor is… *wrong*. It began as standard Ebony Plate, but he has transmuted sections of it *into his body*. The chestplate fuses with his ribcage. The pauldrons merge with his shoulders. Four additional limbs — skeletal arms made of wire, bone, and embedded runestones — emerge from ports in his back where there should be solid metal. The armor breathes with him.

**Helmet:**
A closed sallet helm that appears normal at first glance — until one notices the engraving of **Dethros** has been carved not into the metal, but into Brycen’s exposed skull beneath, visible through gaps where the helm has been *grown into* his head. Bone-fire wings frame his temples. The eye sockets of the drake align with his own.

**Combat Philosophy:**
Brycen is the Order’s answer to Lune — proof that the forbidden can be weaponized. Where Lune mastered six pillars through discipline, Brycen achieved three through **surgical self-destruction**. He is what happens when an Echowalker abandons sanity.

- **Transmutation:** He alters terrain in real-time — mud to stone to trap feet, armor softened to clay, air hardened to blades.
- **Artifice:** His grafted limbs contain runestones that fire shrapnel, project blinding flashes, or emit Abjuration pulses to destabilize enemy wards.
- **Necromancy:** He *rots the wounds he inflicts*. Any cut from his claws begins to necrose. He can briefly puppet recently-dead bodies.

He views combat as **surgery**. Every strike is a *correction*.

**Appearance beneath the helm:**
Pale and still very human like. His head is the only part of him that still looks human. Beautiful deep seated green eyes, and flawless skin. His voice is eager, yet curious. Has one eye that is a chroma scanner.
