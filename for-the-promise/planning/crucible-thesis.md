## The Philosophical Core

**Theme:** **[PROPOSED]** A promise is not the words you swore — it is what you
keep carrying after the person you swore it to is gone. The company is named
*Johanna's Promise*; Chapter 1's flashback carries Johanna's words about the
stone, about legacy, about what it means to carry a name. The title "For the
Promise" points the book's argument at the cost of that carrying.

## The Three Strands

### The Burden (Quest Strand)
**Form:** The poisoned contract. The Council wants Johanna's Promise to escort
and guard a team of researchers into the deep forests of Vel'Era — standard
protection work on the surface. Tauvor withholds the truth: the researchers
are looking for a slumbering drake, and the Promise itself delivered the
Wyrshade that created the conditions for finding it. They are being used twice
for the same catastrophe.

### The Fire (Transformation Strand)
**Danger:** Elaine's riftmark. It floods her with the emotional noise of every
room, opens on its own when she crosses thresholds, and reads the fractures
beneath every performance — including the full Council of Thirteen. It puts
weight behind every word she speaks. Damien is afraid of her. Tella's unspoken
truth, whispered by Phoebe: *dangerous to her enemies and to herself.*

### The Core Bond (Constellation Strand)
**Anchor:** The Promise itself — Brunor, Elaine, Frederick, with Phoebe and
Tella holding the home front at Caer Seryth. Under strain from the start:
Brunor still hasn't said the thing Elaine wanted him to say; Frederick covered
for him at the tavern and that debt is still open ("Two ropes. I'd rather
neither, but I'm a quartermaster. I count what's there.").
