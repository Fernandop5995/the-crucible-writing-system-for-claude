# Overview
Eōtria is a world of two biological inheritances. On the continent of Eōs and its adjacent territories, humans are the dominant species — post-Merge, Chroma-touched, politically fractured. On the continent of Ao, the oldest races alive still walk the world: the Svlenaal (elves), the Fae, and the Orcs. These are not races that coexist easily with the human world. Most of them have no intention of doing so.
The races of Ao collectively refer to themselves as Eōtria's true children. The term is not metaphorical. They are biologically distinct from post-Merge humanity in ways that matter at a cosmological level — they cannot be affected by Chroma, they predate the Merge, and their relationship with magic draws from a source that Chroma cannot touch or replicate.

# I. Humans
### Distribution: All four continents — dominant on Eōs, Aethryn, and Iskar. Present but not dominant on Ao.

Biological Status
Humans are the post-Merge species. Before the Merge, humanity existed in a form closer to what the Ao races still are — biologically removed from arcane influence, living in a world where magic existed only in subtle and featureless ways. The Merge changed this permanently.
When Ubil's seed struck the continent of Ao and fractured the laws of nature into the Thirteen Pillars, it did not merely release Chroma into the environment. It altered human biology at a fundamental level. Chroma embedded itself into human genetics across generations. It became heritable. It became normal. What is now called a mage — a person chosen by Chroma, attuned to one of the Thirteen Pillars — is not an anomaly. They are the natural product of a species whose DNA has been rewritten by an external force over nine hundred years.
From the perspective of the Ao races, Chroma is a disease. Not metaphorically — biologically. Humanity was infected by something that originated from Ubil, the cosmic entity whose purpose is the forced hybridisation of worlds and life. Humans did not receive a gift. They were changed without consent by a force that was, in its origins, hostile to the natural world.
Whether this is accurate, or whether it is the ideological framework of a people who have chosen isolation and needed a theology to justify it, is a question the story does not answer cleanly. What is true: Chroma cannot affect the races of Ao. What is also true: the Merge happened, and humans are different because of it.

The Riftmarked — An Ao Perspective
The Riftmarked are, from the perspective of every race on Ao, the most profoundly Chroma-saturated humans alive. Their bodies are fused with it at a soul level. The legendary heroes of Eōs history — the individuals the five kingdoms revere, whose bloodlines the noble houses trace, whose marks the world waits for — are, in the theology of Ao, the most deeply infected human beings to have ever existed. This tension has no current narrative expression. It waits for the moment Ao and Eōs make genuine contact.

Chroma Immunity
Humans cannot affect the races of Ao with Chroma-based magic. This is not a resistance or a ward. It is biological incompatibility. Chroma evolved in response to human biology. It has no mechanism for interacting with something that predates and stands entirely outside its framework.

# II. The Continent of Ao
Ao sits far southwest in open ocean, west of Iskar, accessible only by navigating the Obsidian Sound — a body of water from which no cartographer has successfully returned with usable charts. No reliable map of Ao exists on Eōs. What knowledge Eōsin scholars possess comes from Iskari oral tradition, secondhand Aethryn records of pre-Merge contact, and the fragmented testimony of sailors who survived the Sound's edges without penetrating its interior.
Ao was lost to the wider world for centuries and rediscovered only at the close of Book 5. The mechanism of its disappearance is addressed separately in the Fae entry below. The races of Ao are aware this disappearance occurred. They were not unaware of the outside world during this period — they chose not to engage with it, and the Fae provided the means to make that choice absolute.
Ao is what Eōtria looked like before Ubil's interference. High fantasy encoded in biology, theology, and architecture. A continent where primordial magic is the air itself and Chroma is a foreign contaminant that has never taken root.

# III. The Svlenaal — The Elves
Svlenaal is the elvish word for their own kind. It carries no direct translation into common — the closest approximation is 'the people of the old breath' or simply 'those who were here.' The three subraces of elves are distinguished by their patron god, their environment, and the prefix of their full name, but all identify under the unified Svlenaal designation when speaking of the whole.
The elves are primordial. They predate the Merge, the Chroma fracture, and the formation of the Five Kingdoms. Their cities are not built on histories of conquest and resettlement — they are built on ground their ancestors were on before human civilization on Eōs existed in any recognisable form. This is not pride. It is geology.

Theology — Shared Foundation
All three subraces believe in the full cosmological order — the two Sak'tirī (Vaïve and Zât), the eight Vak'tirī, and the thirteen Chroma deities. However, the elves do not follow them all equally. Each subrace aligns with a single Vak'tirī patron from the renewal half of the cycle. They acknowledge the entropy half and do not worship it. The Chroma deities are acknowledged as real — they are — but are considered downstream consequences of the Merge rather than objects of genuine devotion. On Ao, the thirteen are real but secondary.
Unlike Eōsin religion, which is congregational and institutional, elvish worship is intimate and daily. There are no clergy in the Eōsin sense. There are keepers of tradition, readers of sign, and individuals who dedicate their lives to attunement — but devotion is personal practice, not organised doctrine.

## Ziktururiel'Svlenaal — The Ash Elves
Translation: 'Elves who live and breed with fire.' Common shorthand: Ash Elves, Fire Elves.

Patron Deity
Iyaï — Vak'tirī of Growth. The renewal force of generation and emergence — the thing that pushes upward through destruction. That their patron is Growth and their environment is volcanic is not contradiction. Fire ends. Growth follows. The Ziktururiel'Svlenaal have built an entire civilisation on the truth that what burns is not what dies.

Appearance
Dark-skinned, ranging from deep umber to near-obsidian depending on lineage and proximity to volcanic heat. Their skin carries a faint undertone of ember — in low light or near heat sources, a subtle luminescence becomes visible beneath the surface, as though the body retains warmth independently of the environment. Eyes are typically amber, gold, or deep orange with occasional flecks of red. Hair grows in dense, coarse textures that resist heat damage naturally. They run warm. A Ziktururiel hand is noticeably warmer to the touch than most others.

Habitat and Architecture
The Ash Elves build into the faces of inactive volcanoes. Not beside them — into them. Their cities are carved directly from volcanic rock, tiered along the interior faces of craters and the external slopes of dormant peaks. The architecture uses the volcano's natural geometry — chambers deepen toward heat sources, residential tiers rise toward cooler air, and the oldest structures sit closest to the rock's heart.
Their temples are near or inside active volcanoes. This is not recklessness — it is theology made physical. The most sacred sites are those where the earth is actively doing what Iyaï embodies: pushing something up through heat and pressure. Proximity to active volcanic activity is a mark of spiritual standing, not danger. Their bodies tolerate heat that would kill most other races. Whether this is biological adaptation or Iyaï's influence made flesh is a debate their own theologians have not resolved.

Character
Deliberate. Patient in the way that geological time is patient — not slow-thinking, but unconcerned with urgency that doesn't earn itself. They build things meant to last centuries. They make decisions with the same orientation. A Ziktururiel who commits to something commits to it the way the volcano commits to the rock: completely, and with heat.

## Celturīrel'Svlenaal — The Wood Elves
Translation: 'Elves who live the old way.' Common shorthand: Wood Elves, Forest Elves.

Patron Deity
Thève — Vak'tirī of Continuity. The force that ensures the cycle does not break, that what must persist does. For a people who define themselves by living the old way, a patron of Continuity is the statement of identity made theological: we are not the past preserved in amber. We are the living proof that the old way still works.

Appearance
The traditional pale-skinned elves of classic archetype, though Ao's version carries its own specificity. Their skin is cool-toned — white to silver-pale, occasionally with a faint green or grey undertone in older bloodlines, as though the forest has left a residue. Eyes are typically green, grey, silver, or pale blue. Hair is fine, often light, and grows long as a cultural norm. They are lean and tall, with the particular physical economy of people who move through dense forest as primary environment — nothing wasted, everything functional.

Habitat and Architecture
The Celturīrel'Svlenaal live within Ao's ancient forests. Their construction philosophy resists the word 'building' — they shape what exists rather than imposing new structures. Dwellings grow with trees, canopy walkways follow the natural geometry of the forest's upper reaches, and the oldest settlements are indistinguishable from the forest itself to any eye that doesn't know what to look for. This is not aesthetic choice alone. It is theology. To cut and rebuild is to break continuity. To grow with what is already there is to honour Thève.
The forest they inhabit on Ao is not the same as Vel'Era's silver forest — but it shares a quality that Vel'Era's Druids, if they ever encountered it, would recognise immediately. The trees of this forest are old in a way that records time rather than simply accumulating it.

Relationship to the Druids
The Druidic tradition originates from Ao, and specifically from within Celturīrel'Svlenaal culture. The primordial pathogen through which female Druids are made, the relationship with the planet-goddess Eōtria at a direct and unmediated level, the orientation toward primordial magic rather than Chroma — all of this comes from the forest elves' theological and biological inheritance. The Druids of Vel'Era are, in the deepest historical sense, the wood elves' exported spiritual legacy: a tradition that survived the crossing to Eōs and embedded itself into the silver forest, which is itself Eōtrian primordial substrate.
The Celturīrel'Svlenaal are aware the Druidic tradition exists elsewhere. They do not consider it a corruption or a dilution. They consider it proof that what Thève embodies is true: some things continue whether you tend them or not.

Character
Reserved in the way of people who have enough history that new things are rarely genuinely new. They observe before acting, speak with precision rather than abundance, and maintain a cultural orientation toward what endures over what arrives. They are not cold — they are simply operating on a longer timescale than most, and courtesy across that gap requires patience from both sides.

## Slqlin'Svlenaal — The Sea Elves
Translation: 'Elves of water.' Common shorthand: Sea Elves, Tideborn.

Patron Deity
Ondë — Vak'tirī of Preservation. The force that holds things in their form against pressure, dissolution, entropy. That the Sea Elves worship Preservation while living beneath the most hostile body of water in the world is the theology expressing itself through habitat: to exist in the Obsidian Sound, you must embody the thing that resists being unmade.

Appearance
The most visually distinct of the three subraces and the furthest from any human baseline. Blue-skinned, in ranges that span from pale ice-blue through deep indigo to near-black at the deepest tidal lineages. The variation tracks with depth of habitual dwelling — shallower communities tend toward lighter blues, the oldest deepwater settlements produce the darkest. Their eyes are large relative to face size, adapted for low-light underwater environments, and are typically silver, white, or a very pale blue that reads as almost colourless in surface light. Gills are present alongside functional lungs. Their limbs are longer in proportion to their bodies than the other subraces, and their hands carry webbing between the fingers that retracts partially in air but never fully disappears.
Among the Svlenaal, the Slqlin are the ones most immediately identifiable as categorically different from anything on Eōs. A Wood Elf or Ash Elf seen in poor light or at distance might be mistaken for an unusual human. A Sea Elf would not be.

Habitat
They live beneath the Obsidian Sound. Their cities exist in the deep water — carved into underwater geological formations, built around hydrothermal vents, nested inside the ruins of things that sank into the Sound before recorded history. The Obsidian Sound's reputation as an impenetrable, ship-swallowing body of water is accurate from the surface perspective. From below, it is a civilisation.
The relationship between the Sea Elves and the Sound's hostility to surface navigation is not accidental. The Sound's dangers — crushing riptides, black water, arcane leviathans — are partially natural and partially maintained. The Slqlin'Svlenaal are not the source of these dangers, but they have never worked to reduce them either. The Sound is their perimeter. They have not chosen to open it.

Character
The Sea Elves are the most isolated of the three subraces, including from the other Svlenaal. The physical separation of living underwater from living above it produces a culture that has evolved with less cross-pollination. They are not hostile to the other subraces — family is family — but they operate with a degree of remove that the others acknowledge and respect without fully closing. Communication happens. Council happens. But the Slqlin bring the weight of the deep with them into every exchange, and it shows.

# IV. The Orcs — The Corrupted Svlenaal
The orcs are not a separate race. They are elves.
Specifically: they are what remains of a Svlenaal lineage that bound itself too completely to Vekh — the Vak'tirī of Adaptation — at some point before recorded elvish history. The precise event is not preserved in any canon-accessible document. What the other elves know, and what the orcs carry in their body and their theology, is the shape of what happened: Vekh is dead. The god they gave themselves to no longer exists as a coherent divine entity. The power they channelled through that devotion had nowhere clean to go when its source shattered. It adapted. It took what was elvish and remade it into something that could survive on a dead god's residue.

Appearance
Larger than any of the three Svlenaal subraces. Broad-built, dense-boned, their skin running from grey-green to deep olive to near-brown depending on lineage. Tusks are present in most lineages, upper canines extending past the lip line, more pronounced in older bloodlines. Their eyes carry a dull luminescence — not the clean warmth of the Ash Elves' ember-glow, but something murkier, as though the light is old and hasn't been refreshed. They are not monstrous in the way of something designed for horror. They are recognisably the result of a beautiful thing being fundamentally altered by grief.

Theology
The orcs worship Vekh. Not as a living god — they know Vekh is dead. They worship what Vekh was, and they carry the adaptation that Vekh's death forced on their ancestors as a form of devotion. Their theology is the most honest of any race on Ao: they do not pretend their god lives, they do not claim their corruption is a blessing, and they do not expect deliverance. They adapt because they must. They honour Vekh because Vekh made them into something that could survive when it no longer could.
The other elves do not despise the orcs. They grieve them, which the orcs find worse. The orcs do not want to be grieved. They want to be left alone, which is largely what they have.

Distribution and Character
Rare. Solitary in the way of things that have learned that proximity to others ends in one kind of pain or another. They live in seclusion across Ao's harder terrain — the edges of the volcanic regions, the deep forest margins where neither the Ash nor Wood Elves maintain regular presence, the rocky coastal strips above the waterline. They are not aggressive unless threatened, and they define 'threatened' broadly enough that most other races have learned to give them distance and receive it in return.
They are not unintelligent. They are not savage. They are people carrying a wound that is now several thousand years old and has become structural. The wound is the shape of who they are.

# V. The Fae
The Fae are the second most dominant species on Ao and the most dangerous thing that lives on it.
They coexist with the Svlenaal. This coexistence is close — the Fae and the elves know each other, live near each other, share council on matters of continental significance, and have done so for long enough that the distinction between political alliance and simply being neighbours has blurred in ways neither side fully tracks. This proximity should not be mistaken for safety. The Fae are not domesticated by familiarity. They are merely familiar.

Appearance
Variable in ways that resist clean description. The Fae do not have a single consistent physical form the way the elf subraces do. They present in a range from nearly-elvish to something that has clearly never been trying to resemble anything human or humanoid. The common thread is a quality of wrongness at the edges — details that do not resolve correctly in peripheral vision, proportions that shift between observation and recall, a sense that what you are looking at is a choice the Fae is making about what to show you rather than what they actually are.
Their most consistent feature is age. Even the youngest-presenting Fae carries something in the eyes or the stillness of the body that reads as older than the context allows.

Magic — Primordial, Not Chroma
The Fae are true harnesses of Primordial magic. Their spells do not draw from Chroma and cannot be countered, nullified, or understood through any Chroma-based magical framework. The gap between their most powerful workings and the most powerful Chroma magic is not a question of degree. It is a question of category.
Chroma is a fracture — thirteen pieces of something that was once unified, now distributed unevenly through a world that was forced to absorb it. Primordial magic is what existed before the fracture, drawing from the substrate that preceded everything Chroma built on top of. The Fae access the substrate. They do not work within the system. They work at the level below the system's foundations.
If a fully capable Fae mage ever reached Eōs and chose to act, no existing magical institution on the continent would have a meaningful framework for responding. The Caltheran Council, the Runescribe League, the Echoa Archive — none of them have encountered, studied, or theorised anything that would prepare them for this. The threat category does not exist in their academic literature because nothing on Eōs has ever presented it.

Theology — The Sak'tirī
The Fae worship Vaïve (Chaos) and Zât (Order) — the two First Gods, the Sak'tirī — as the foundational divine reality above all others. They acknowledge the Vak'tirī and the Chroma deities as real. They do not worship them. The lesser divine hierarchy is downstream from what the Fae consider the only entities worth orienting a spiritual life toward.
Their understanding of the Sak'tirī is more accurate than any Eōsin theological tradition. Vaïve and Zât did not die in the way mortals die, or even in the way Asthœl died. They completed their function — they brought existence into being, they established the conditions for the Vak'tirī cycle to operate, and their essence dissolved into the structure of what they created. Chaos and Order are not gone. They are the universe's architecture. To worship them is to orient yourself toward the fabric of everything rather than toward any of its expressions.
This is why Fae Primordial magic is what it is. They are not drawing on a dead god's residue like the orcs, or on a living deity's attention like the Eōsin traditions. They are drawing on the substrate of existence itself, which does not deplete, does not fluctuate with divine mood, and does not care whether the person accessing it is worthy. It simply is. And the Fae know how to reach it.

The Disappearance of Ao — Canon Note
Ao was inaccessible to the wider world for centuries before its rediscovery at the close of Book 5. The mechanism of its disappearance involves the Chronurgy rift on Ao — Imarasil's domain — and a working cast by the Fae using that rift's power. The continent did not move. It was temporally obscured. Every attempt to find it placed a navigator in the right location at the wrong version of the moment. The Obsidian Sound, already impassable under normal conditions, became additionally unreachable through a working that bent time around the edges of the approach.
The Fae's motivation for this action is currently unresolved canon. Whether they acted to protect Ao, to protect the world from Ao, or out of motivations that do not map cleanly onto either category is an open question. Their own accounting of it, if one were ever offered, would probably be accurate and not fully comprehensible to anyone who asked.
The reappearance at the close of Book 5 — whether the working expired, was lifted, or was disrupted by something else — is also unresolved and will be addressed in Book 8.

Distribution and Intent
The Fae have no current interest in Eōs, Aethryn, or Iskar. They know these places exist. They have known for a long time. They choose not to engage, and they have the means to make that choice hold against most pressures. What would change this calculus is unknown. What is known is that a Fae who has decided to act on Eōs would represent a category of event for which the continent has no preparation and no precedent.

# VI. Canon Locks and Standing Rules
Svlenaal is the elvish word for their own kind, used across all subraces as a unified designation.

The orcs are corrupted Svlenaal, transformed by the death of Vekh. They worship Vekh as a dead god. They are not a separate species — they are what one elvish lineage became.
The Fae worship the Sak'tirī exclusively. Their Primordial magic draws from the substrate of existence and cannot be countered by Chroma-based means.
Chroma is encoded as biological contamination in the DNA of post-Merge humans. This is the Ao perspective and is factually accurate in the world's cosmological terms, though not the framework humans use for themselves.
The Riftmarked are, from an Ao perspective, the most Chroma-saturated humans alive. No current narrative expression. Flag for future use.
No reliable map of Ao exists on Eōs. No cartographer has successfully charted the Obsidian Sound. What knowledge exists is Iskari oral tradition and pre-Merge Aethryn records.

I. EOTRIAN — THE COMMON TONGUE

Eotrian is the shared language of the five kingdoms of Eōs, the continent of Aethryn, and the Iskari communities that have lived within Eōsin borders long enough to require it. It is not a constructed diplomatic language — it evolved organically from the pre-Merge linguistic substrate of the continent’s early civilisations, stabilised over the First through Third Eras as the five kingdoms consolidated, and has served as the continent’s working tongue ever since.

The neutral standard — the accent against which all others are measured — is Halreth speech. This is not arbitrary. Halreth’s position as Eōs’s diplomatic centre, combined with its role as the continent’s primary food supplier and its deliberate cultivation of pan-kingdom accessibility, made its accent the default of formal communication, institutional writing, and educated speech across the continent. A student at the ENAA trying to neutralise a regional accent is approximating Halreth. A trade document written to be read across all five kingdoms reads like Halreth. A king receiving foreign ambassadors speaks Halreth-standard regardless of where he was born.

Education is the primary flattening force. Nobility and the educated professional class speak with significantly reduced regional markers. A Varkaan-born noble who attended the ENAA sounds nothing like a Varkaan farmhand. The accent reduction is itself a class signal — smooth speech indicates formation, rough speech indicates its absence. This is understood by everyone and acknowledged by almost no one.

Regional Accents:

Halreth — The neutral standard. Balanced vowels, moderate pace, consonants neither clipped nor drawn. The accent of institutions, diplomacy, and the educated class continent-wide. Regional Halreth — the farming communities of the Vespur Plains interior, the maritime settlements of Old Thenbury — carries warmth and slight vowel lengthening that educated Halreth irons out.

Varkaan — The hardest accent on the continent. Germanic and Norse in character. Consonants are struck rather than shaped — stops are clean and terminal, fricatives carry weight. Vowels are compressed, clipped at the edges. Varkaan speech sounds like decisions being made. The highland communities and Iron Guild workers carry the thickest version. Noble and educated Varkaan retains the consonant precision while moderating the vowel compression. The result is an accent that sounds controlled rather than rough — which is how Varkaan prefers to present itself.

Caltheran — Greek-adjacent, and for good reason. The Aethryn settler migration around 400 A.M. embedded an Aethryn linguistic substrate into Caltheran’s developing speech patterns that five centuries of distance has softened but never erased. Caltheran speech is characterised by open vowels, rolling consonant transitions, and a cadence that tends toward the rhetorical — sentences that feel shaped for argument rather than simple communication. An educated Caltheran speaker sounds like someone who has considered what they are about to say before they say it, even when they haven’t. The Echoa Archive’s academic register is the formal version of this. The common Caltheran accent in Glintgate or Lentorum is the same substrate without the polish.

Naaren — Byzantine in character. Eastern European in feel — something between Russian and Turkish in its consonant weight and vowel depth. Naaren speech moves from the back of the mouth more than any other Eōsin accent. Certain consonants that other kingdoms pronounce forward are produced further back in the throat. The effect is an accent that sounds guarded even in casual speech, which suits Naaren’s institutional character well. The Cipher’s Guild speaks in a cultivated version of this — precise, unhurried, giving nothing away in tone that the words don’t explicitly provide.

Vel’Era — Celtic in character, Welsh and Irish in specific texture. Vel’Era speech lilts. It moves up and down in pitch across sentences in a pattern that other kingdoms sometimes misread as emotional when it is simply how the language flows in that mouth. Consonant clusters soften at the edges. Certain vowels that Halreth pronounces flat are rounded in Vel’Era speech. The old-ways communities of the interior carry the thickest version. Coastal Vel’Era, exposed to more continental trade traffic, sounds somewhat flattened by comparison.

Aethryn — Eotrian with an Aethryn accent sounds like the original version of what Caltheran domesticated. The Greek substrate is present but rawer, less integrated with the Eōsin phonological baseline. The additional quality — the Australian inflection in its cadence — produces rising intonation at sentence ends that reads to Eōsin ears as perpetually questioning. Aethryn speakers are aware this impression exists and find it mildly irritating. The accent is immediately distinctive. An Aethryn-born speaker in Caltheran sounds like Caltheran’s grandfather.

II. IXESE — THE ISKARI TONGUE

Ixese is a full language, not a dialect. It predates Eotrian common and predates the formation of the five kingdoms. The Iskari brought it with them when they settled the Sol’telarī centuries before Varkaan existed as a political entity, and it has survived the subsequent centuries of Varkaan administrative pressure, institutional marginalisation, and cultural erasure because it was never simply a communication tool. It is the Iskari’s primary vessel for theological knowledge, oral history, and cultural identity. You cannot replace it with Eotrian without losing everything it contains that Eotrian has no words for.

Phonologically, Ixese operates on a different vowel system than Eotrian. The vowels are placed differently in the mouth — more fronted, with a compressed quality that produces the consonant cluster effects audible when Iskari speakers use Eotrian. The back-of-tongue elevation that Kai identifies in the prefect’s pronunciation of by — “the back of the tongue was higher than Eotrian requires” — is Ixese phonology bleeding through the surface of a second language. Fluent bilingual Iskari speakers carry the substrate. It never fully leaves.

Ixese uses different vowels than Eotrian at a structural level. Names and terms drawn from Ixese carry this distinction — the vowel patterns don’t map cleanly onto Eotrian orthography, which is why Iskari names often read as phonetically distinct from Eōsin names even in written form.

The language is currently endangered on Eōs. The generation of Iskari who grew up entirely within Varkaan’s administrative structure speak Eotrian as a first language and Ixese as a heritage tongue. Their children may not speak it at all. The women of Kye’vathn who conduct the old rites, who tend the desert ecology, who carry the oral tradition — they are Ixese’s last fluent native speakers on the continent. This is not unrelated to the purge proposal being debated in Varkaan’s council chambers.

III. SVLENAAL — THE ELVISH TONGUE

The Svlenaal language is primordial. It did not evolve alongside a civilisation — it evolved alongside a people who existed before the Merge, before Chroma, before the human political structures that produced Eotrian. It has no known parent language and no confirmed relationship to any tongue spoken on Eōs, Aethryn, or Iskar.

No reliable written record of Svlenaal exists outside Ao. What is known comes from the conlang’s internal structure, recoverable from elvish names and titles that have survived in Iskari oral tradition and pre-Merge Aethryn records.

Phonology:

Svlenaal permits initial consonant clusters that Eotrian does not — Slq-, Zik-, Svl- are all valid word openings. This is the most immediately foreign quality of the language to any Eōsin ear. The phonological onset rules that govern Eotrian and most human languages simply do not apply.

Long vowels, marked with macrons (ā, ī, ū), carry semantic weight distinct from their short counterparts. The difference between Celturī- and Celturi- would be a difference of meaning, not simply of duration.

The apostrophe marks a compound construction — a connector particle that joins a descriptive stem to a head noun. The relationship it encodes is something between possession and category membership: Ziktururiel’Svlenaal is not “the Svlenaal of Ziktururiel” but rather “those who are Svlenaal through being Ziktururiel.” The distinction matters. The apostrophe construction is not ownership. It is ontological category.

Vowel Harmony — The Suffix Rule:

The most recoverable grammatical rule in Svlenaal is the behaviour of the nominal suffix -rel/-iel. These are not two separate morphemes. They are one suffix governed by vowel harmony with the final syllable of the stem it attaches to.

Front vowels (ī, e, i) in the final stem syllable take -rel:
Celturī- → Celturīrel

Back and rounded vowels (u, o, a) in the final stem syllable take -iel:
Zikturur- → Ziktururiel

This rule is consistent across all recovered examples and produces a naturalistic phonological alternation. The suffix itself likely functions as a nominal class marker — indicating that what precedes it is a category of being rather than a simple noun.

Svlenaal itself does not take the suffix. It is the archaic root form — the word for the people before the suffix system existed or before it applied to the people themselves. It is the name, not the description.

Slqlin similarly does not take the suffix, suggesting it belongs to a different or older word class. The sea elves’ name for themselves may predate the suffix system entirely, preserved in its archaic form because the Slqlin’Svlenaal’s deep isolation produced a conservative linguistic culture. What the oldest forms of Svlenaal looked like before the suffix system developed is unknown.

Core Recoverable Vocabulary:

IV. STANDING NOTES

Eotrian is the named common tongue. Halreth accent is the neutral standard. All other regional accents deviate from it in documented directions.

Ixese predates Eotrian and is a full separate language. It is currently endangered on Eōs due to Varkaan’s administrative and cultural pressure on Iskari communities.

Svlenaal is primordial, unrelated to any human tongue, and has no reliable external written record. The grammar base above is recoverable from canonical names and should be treated as the foundation for future conlang development rather than a complete system.

Aethryn common is Eotrian with a distinct accent — the source substrate for Caltheran’s Greek-adjacent quality, now heard in its rawer original form.

The elvish dead god names are an open thread. Do not invent them without authorial decision.
