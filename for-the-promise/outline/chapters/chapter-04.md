# Chapter 4 Outline: The Duel (Brunor)

**Status:** WRITTEN (~3,740 words) | **Date:** 8th of Ashenfall, evening —
Bordou Square, in the rain

## Scene 1 — The contest of arms
- Reading Damien: Caltherian court fencing over a spearman's instincts;
  Uthred's voice ("A man's first three steps tell you the rest")
- First blood to Damien — a thin line across Brunor's forearm, displayed to
  the crowd
- The psychological warfare, in order: Uthred ("The Halvyr legacy, and he
  bleeds it out on my flagstones"), Elaine ("Do you collect broken things..."),
  then Alessa — the mud, the men opened one by one, the implication Damien
  lets hang. Brunor charges.
- The brawl: headbutt, split lip, the stolen pirouette (Elaine's, Tella's
  before that); Nullbrand hums against Damien's enchanted gambeson
- The final exchange declared — killing blows — and **Elaine between them**:
  her own blade holding Nullbrand, her dagger catching the golden plunge; she
  turns both men out of the bind
- The grey-hooded woman by the gates: unmoved by blood, a tilt of the head,
  gone. (Why Elaine truly intervened — held for Ch 5.)
- Damien's quiet words to Elaine that no one else hears; her color goes
- "Golden Scales guildhall. By week's end. Do try not to fuck up twice."

## Scene 2 — The alley / Candler's Row / the crossroads
- The argument: "A coin flip in the air!" vs "There are things more important
  than my life"; "You don't think I'd have won." / "I think I wasn't going to
  find out."
- The Glintgate rant (rats, white stones, six carps, Hans and the necklace)
- The Alessa reckoning begins: two months hidden; Ferrena's scouts silenced —
  by Brunor, which he cannot say; the Vigil context (three weeks surrounded by
  the Order, the Kymancy mark, Phoebe, the Hollow, Tella, "that damned rift
  hovering above it all"); the spy investigation precedent; Merea told during
  "the security appraisal" — "code"
- "*You* are suffocating me, Brunor."; the Merea memory (her quarters,
  "congratulations," the doll learning emotion)
- Stonefield tries to surface; he fights the name down
- Frederick arrives out of the rain: "what in the gods' names happened to
  your face, man?" (hook into Ch 5)

## Function
The duel pays off Ch 2; Brunor's suppression of the Alessa reports exposed as
the fracture line; Damien's unheard words planted; Celisia sighted; the trio
reassembled at its lowest point of trust.
